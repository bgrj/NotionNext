## About Our Beings

**Our Beings** is not a blog in the conventional sense.
It is closer to a cyber garden built for ordinary people, and a digital archive designed to resist time and oblivion.

What I want to do here is more than writing, more than sharing tools, and more than expressing myself.

I want to preserve what is easy to be overlooked, buried, and lost over time.
- the thoughts of an ordinary person;
- the story of an ordinary person;
- a trace of a life that truly lived;
- a life experience that is not fully described but definitely existed.

This place believes in one thing:

> It is not only the great, the successful, or the victorious who deserve to be recorded.
> Every person who has truly lived, thought, and struggled deserves to leave something in the world.

---

## Why it exists

Human life ultimately revolves around two words: **Dao** and **Shu**.

### Dao (Why)

**Dao** is the reason for being.
- Why live? Why learn? Why endure? Why pursue? Why feel unwilling?
- Who are you? What do you want to become?
- How do your era, society, family, and rules affect you?

Dao is not empty talk.
It is a deep perception of yourself and the world. Starting from Dao, you can understand where to go.

### Shu (How)

**Shu** is the practical toolkit.
- Paths and methods for exams and skill improvement
- Foreign language learning strategies
- Content creation and website building experience
- Productivity tools and high-value resource curation

Shu helps people take action, solve problems, and reach goals.
Without Dao, Shu can easily become pointless busyness.

### A bridge between Dao and Shu

**Our Beings** aims to build a bridge between Dao and Shu:
- Dao helps you understand why;
- Shu helps you move steadily;
- let Shu serve Dao, rather than turning life into an endless treadmill.

Even if you cannot fully understand it right away, that is okay.
Some things should not be fully understood immediately.
At least, the question can be planted and quietly remain in your heart as a seed.

---

## Dao and Shu

In **Our Beings**, Dao is not empty talk, Shu is not a checklist.
Both must be anchored in everyday life.

### About Dao

Dao focuses on these more fundamental questions:
- How does a person understand their life?
- How does a person face birth, aging, sickness, death, love, hate, desire, and loss?
- How does a person position themself between society structure, family responsibility, era rules, and personal will?
- How does a person, in an imperfect reality, still try to confirm "who am I"?

Topics like ["the game through most ordinary Chinese people's lives"](https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/ZLycAFwJ8oY1Ra9Qh4w3JQ) are essentially talking about Dao.
The true question is not a specific skill,
but:
- why are most people driven by a certain life script?
- why are they constantly running between education, work, housing, parenting, retirement, respect, and security?
- why is it hard to truly leave even when exhausted?

When a person begins to see these things, they may slowly realize:
- what they pursue may not just be success;
- what they fear may not just be failure;
- what they desperately hold on to might be a way of life that can still be explained to family and self.

### About Shu

Shu is the toolkit in the real world.
It may include:
- Exam and education guides
- Foreign language learning methods
- Site building and content creation experience
- High-value resource curation
- Practical tools to improve efficiency, gain ability, and reduce trial and error

These are essential.
If one only talks Dao without Shu, it can stay at feelings and self-comfort.
If one has only Shu without Dao, they may lose sight of why they started in endless competition.

So what this site truly wants is not to fall on one side,
but to let them illuminate each other:
- Use Dao to help people understand why they live;
- Use Shu to help people act concretely;
- Use Shu back to serve Dao, not trap people in busy work.

## What I hope it brings

I hope **Our Beings** leaves visitors with at least three things.

### 1. Light

To make people realize:
Many confusions are not only personal problems.
They often come from the shadows of era, structure, family, education, comparison, and fear.

### 2. Method

After understanding why, this site can provide practical Shu:
No exaggeration, no deification, no illusion—just useful, actionable paths.

### 3. Seeds

Even if you don't have answers today, this site hopes to plant questions in your heart.
They may not change you immediately, but possibly grow into new understanding of yourself in the future.

## If you are here

If you are in one of these situations, this place might be useful for you:

- You are studying lots of "Shu" but increasingly uncertain why you are learning them
- You want to understand the predicament of ordinary people, society structure, and life scripts
- You want deeper humanistic and thoughtful content, not just fragmented information
- You need some truly usable methods, materials, resources, and practical experience
- You want to leave something in the world, even if it's just a tiny trace
- You believe that the existence of ordinary people itself is worth recording, seeing, and passing on

## Why Ordinary People

Because I always believe the world should not only write histories for those who are seen.
More often, the silent ones are ordinary people, and the forgotten ones are also ordinary people.

They don't have grand narratives, but they have thoughts, experience, judgment, failure, love, shame, hope, and the strength to hold life.

**Our Beings** wants to reserve a place for ordinary people's voices and stories:
- not defining anyone;
- recognizing every real existence;
- making each life moment visible.

Ordinary people's thoughts, stories, and existence itself deserve to be carefully preserved.

## How You Can Participate

Although **Our Beings** was started by me, I don't want it to remain only my space.
I hope it becomes an open archive of existence, a place where more ordinary people can leave traces.

### 1. Leave a trace

This is the lightest, and also the most important way to participate.

You can:
- Leave a valuable comment or message
- Record your genuine thoughts and perspective
- Share a segment of your experience or judgment
- Add your methods and experience under an article
- Offer different perspectives on a question

Sometimes, one carefully written comment itself becomes value that future readers will see.

### 2. Contribute content

If you are willing to co-create content with us, you can participate in these ways:

- Submit articles, notes, essays, letters, short reviews
- Provide resources curated on a particular topic
- Share a methodology you have verified
- Tell your ordinary life story, family experience, social observation, and survival experience

Possible contribution topics include but are not limited to:

- Life experience of ordinary people
- Learning and growth paths
- Foreign language, writing, site building, expression
- County towns, villages, cities, and mobile living
- Family responsibility, education competition, marriage and child-rearing, and midlife predicament
- Thoughts, tools, and stories worth preserving

### 3. Contribute ideas and clues

If you don't want to write content directly right now, you can contribute "directions".

You can provide:
- Topic suggestions
- Website structure and page suggestions
- Thematic planning ideas
- Resource types or themes you wish to see curated
- People, stories, problems, and phenomena worth recording

### 4. Help with proofreading, organization, and editing

Many projects lack not only authors but also patient organizers.

You can help with:
- Proofreading spelling and phrasing
- Optimizing layout structure
- Organizing contents and tags
- Assisting with thematic archiving
- Connecting related articles
- Providing feedback on broken links and page experience

### 5. Help with spreading

If you think an article or method might help others, welcome to pass it on.

You can:
- Share and repost articles
- Recommend the site to friends
- Bookmark and keep following
- Quote, discuss, and extend on social platforms
- Pass on the value here in your own way

### 6. Resource exchange and support

Not everything must be done with money. You can also support each other through resource circulation:

- Exchange or donate unused books
- Share learning materials and notes
- Provide creation tool support (recording devices, old cameras, microphones, stands, etc.)
- Exchange digital resources (templates, fonts, materials, etc.)
- Help with domains, server fees, tool memberships, or other resource support

### 7. Provide skill support

If you have certain skills, welcome to directly contribute them to website building:

- Translation
- Design, illustration, cover design
- Frontend / Backend / DevOps
- SEO and content structure suggestions
- Audio editing, video production, subtitle organization
- Legal, copyright, and agreement advice
- Data organization and research assistance

### 8. Be a long-term companion

You can also do nothing urgent right now, just stay, read, bookmark, and follow.
Being a long-term companion itself is a form of support.

Some projects aren't built on one-time enthusiasm,
but by a group of people quietly, persistently, and genuinely letting it grow.
